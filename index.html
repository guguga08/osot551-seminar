Guiding question

How is AI shaping healthcare delivery, professional decision-making, and OT practice — and what role should occupational therapists play in its integration?

01

Learning objectives

By the end of the session, each of us should be able to hold these five capabilities — not as facts to recall, but as lenses to think with.

01

Describe current applications of AI within healthcare and rehabilitation.

02

Critically examine ethical concerns — bias, privacy, accountability, and equity.

03

Explore tensions between AI-assisted practice and OT clinical reasoning.

04

Analyze the benefits and limitations of AI as a treatment and rehabilitation tool.

05

Consider how OTs can contribute to ethical, client-centred AI implementation.

Small-group scenarios

Three cases, three frictions. Each group takes one. Read the case aloud, start the timer, then reveal the questions one at a time — let each one breathe before moving on.
